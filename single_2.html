I say the love between couples, between parent and child and between siblings doesn't exist, it’s just that humans have evolved and developed it in our genes just for the sake of continuation of species...

That's just about it. You love your parents because you have originated from them. They are your creators. Parents love you and care for you because you are their offspring and your survival means species continuation and love between siblings because they are originated from same parents and share same genes… When early men would visit a watering hole if they didn't watch their child and it got snatched by a crocodile our brains would create a painful but instructional memory so it would never happen again.

This rage, love, emotion it's not real it's a survival Instinct wired deep into our brains to ensure continuation of species for which I am cleared of. I admit emotions like these enable us to form societies and live in harmony but tell me what worth does human life have? 10rs? There are poisons available easily for less than a dollar or two which would… you know…

People act according to their perceptions and impulses.

Meaning if a person comes back to you consciously, it’s because it benefits him in some way, but this doesn’t mean you don’t get satisfaction when he comes to see you too, and you feel happy because he feels happy (maybe) and vice versa. Even though we can’t really perform what we call altruism, as in, be the other person, we can act with the other person’s well-being in mind over own. If not then how could a mother make the conscious decision of dying for her child? The fact that people only act according to their internal impulse doesn’t go against empathy or how society works, it’s one of the most beautiful things about life. We are more of a very complicated system than a set of individuals, in simple words, we use each other for our species to subsist. Take friendship for example.

If you need companionship, you find someone to befriend. You are then using that person to fill that need. It doesn’t make you a bad person (unless you are intentionally a bad friend, of course), nor does it mean that using someone in that context is bad, but it is what it is.
